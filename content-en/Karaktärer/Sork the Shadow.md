Orc Rogue. Jumped ship in Fireshear, overheard [[Nasher]] and [[Zahir]] talking about heading north and invited himself along. [[Borc the Orc]] was looking for travel companions north.

- Excellent Survival and Perception. Skilled navigator but lost his bearing toward [[Kelvins Cairn]] (~2 extra hours, Day 18).

- Bungled his first long rest (attempted theft, 25 gp fine, only had 11 gp). [[Zahir]] covered half.

## Background

Grew up in the shadow of a barracks in Neverwinter. As a child he helped human soldiers with cooking and cleaning — at night, when the camp fell silent, he would sneak off to "play" with the training equipment. Over the years he developed a natural talent for combat and impressive strength.

His talent did not go unnoticed. He was invited to enlist — the only orc in a unit of humans. He served for several years with great success, rising through the ranks alongside men he considered brothers.

He later made his way to Waterdeep and worked as a bodyguard for the nobleman **Lord Cassalanter**. It did not take long for Sork to realize that the nobleman was deeply corrupt. When he witnessed Lord Cassalanter brutalizing his servants, something snapped — in front of everyone, Sork pressed a dagger to the nobleman's throat. He never delivered the killing blow, but the message was unmistakable.

The confrontation made Sork a hunted man by the city watch and forced him underground into Waterdeep's slums. This is the "bad situation in Waterdeep" that [[Nasher]] has noticed Sork refuses to talk about.

He fled on a merchant ship heading north to escape the gallows. He ended up in [[Fireshear]] by pure chance and desperation — a place where no one knows his face or his past. That is where he ran into [[Nasher]] and [[Zahir]].

## Personality

Utterly hopeless in social situations — but can't keep quiet and chimes in anyway, usually at the wrong moment. Happiest out in the wilderness or when scouting and investigating things; that is where he feels useful and in his element.

## Moral Code

Easily excited when valuables are found. After the incident in [[Bryn Shander]] he has set himself one rule: **only steal from those who no longer need it.**

In practice:
- Took gems (~20 gp) from [[Bronn Bearhammer(†)]]'s iron chest — Bronn is dead and has no need of them.
- Left [[Keegan Valryn]] and [[Garret]]'s belongings untouched — they may still be alive.
- Chose *not* to pay [[Jarthra]] with Bronn's gems — she knew Bronn and might have recognized his property.
- May trade with the dwarves in [[Dvärgadalen]] instead.
